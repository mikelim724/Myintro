College and After:

After highschool, I began college at Northwestern University in the fall of 2014. I initially enrolled into the university with Biological Sciences as my major. I also wanted to get a major in Computer Science, but I realized that I would not be able to fit in all the neccesary classes for my Biological Sciences major along with the CS classes. I went on to finish most of my Bio classes by senior year and decided to take a couple CS classes for my senior year. Again, I faced many long nights coding, but the gratification I got from completing a project felt just as good as the first day in high school. It reminded me of the passion I had and wanted to get a minor if possible. Unfortunately, I could not finish the minor without taking 3 more classes the next quarter. However, my financial aid ran out and would have to pay around $12,000. I decided to just graduate with my Biology degree and study for the MCAT after I graduated. After completing the MCAT this past June, I sat down and had to evaluate my life. I could either choose to to apply this cycle or hold off and see what I wanted to do. All the studying I did just completely drained me, and I was just tired of science material. I thought back to the last time I actually enjoyed learning material, and it led me back to computer science. I always wanted to finish my minor, but without financial aid it would not be worth it unless it was a major. That is when I did research into bootcamps and looked up all the reviews I could on switchup and course report.
